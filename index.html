The following heatmaps classify Austrian ski resorts based on the behavior of the resorts' hotel businesses in managing weather risk. One heatmap measures the firms' exposure to weather shocks while the other measures their inflexibility in adapting to changes in weather risk by reducing their operating leverage. All data points correspond to an Austrian district and result from information about at least 18 firms. We only consider districts which contain a municipality less than 25 km from a ski resort according to Wikipedia's list of Austrian ski resorts.

  • Exposure heatmap.

    This heatmap depicts a measure of hotel' businesses exposure to snow risk. The heatmap shows an altitude-adjusted measure of exposure comparing the effects of snow shocks on hotel businesses in ski resorts at similar levels of altitude.

    Similar heatmaps can be constructed for other industries based on other measures of weather risk. The heatmaps can also be used to compare regions in terms of the effects of climate change on firms in a given industry.
     
  • Inflexibility heatmaps.

    These heatmaps depict how the ski resorts' hotel businesses adapt to an increase in weather risk by reducing their operating leverage. We use a "movie" of heatmaps to show that the firms change their behavior, - their inflexibility decreases over time. This trend may be specific to the hotel industry in ski resorts as an industry affected by climate change in a particularly straightforward way.

    Similar heatmaps can be constructed for other industries.

The data behind the heatmaps are publicly available. Follow this link to obtain measures of the hotel businesses' weather exposure (WE) and adaptation inflexibility (AI), adjusted for the altitude of ski resorts. The first measure is depicted in the exposure heatmap while the second is depicted in the inflexibility heatmaps.

Baumgartner and Stomper (2020) show that the heatmaps can be used to predict the number of hospitality businesses filing for bankruptcy in a given region. This is possible even though the heapmaps are not based on any data about bankruptcies.
